Among them was the Clever Merchant, their respected Leader, whose quick wit was as renowned as his exquisite silks. As the afternoon sun beat down relentlessly, a fearsome cry split the air. From behind a thicket of thorny scrub, three burly figures emerged, the notorious robbers who styled themselves the "Three Monarchs" of the highway. With menacing clubs and glinting daggers, they ambushed the unsuspecting traders, swiftly disarming them, stripping them of their rich turbans, fine kurtas, and even their humble dhotis, leaving them bare and vulnerable on the dusty ground beside their plundered wares.}
{Drunk on their sudden victory and the heaps of precious cloth and coin, the Three Robbers began to celebrate, their crude laughter echoing across the barren landscape. Their leader, a corpulent man with a scarred face, leered at the shivering merchants. "Ha! What a pitiful sight!" he bellowed. "But even plucked pigeons can dance, can they not? Amuse us, you wretched traders! Dance for your masters!" And so, with taunts and prods, the robbers forced the humiliated merchants to perform a grotesque dance, their bare feet shuffling awkwardly in the dust, their hearts heavy with despair and the sting of injustice.}
{As the merchants reluctantly capered, their Clever Merchant, the Leader, observed his captors closely. With a deep breath, he began to sing, his voice carrying a melancholic tune that seemed to lament their plight, yet within its rhythm, he wove words understood only by his fellow Traders. "Erith, enty, eno, the wind it blows so low," he crooned, his eyes subtly flicking towards the nearest robber. "Erith, enty, eno, a grip upon the foe!
